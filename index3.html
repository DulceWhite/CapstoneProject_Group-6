Find Your Voice, Floods need action!

Flood Talk recognizes the severity of floods in our community. Our goal is to bring awareness to the issue by giving the people a platform to voice out their stories in order to promote solutions.

According to Corurier Journal Kentucky has some of the highest concentrations of community flood risk in the country, according to a report.
Almost 249,000 properties in the commonwealth "have greater than a 26% chance of being severely affected by flooding over the next 30 years," the "Third National Flood Risk Assessment" says. That's about 13% of all properties statewide, putting Kentucky in the top four of all states in terms of flood risk. Read the article.

Leah's Alarm created by Barren County High School

"Students at Barren County High School have develope an alarm system that will warn users about high water in honor of a student Leah Carter, who died when her car was swept away by flood waters as she tried to cross a bridge on Lyon Road in Monroe County" Read the article.

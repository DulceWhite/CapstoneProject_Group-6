Find Your Voice, Floods need action!

Flood Talk recognizes the severity of floods in our community and the damaged caused. Our goal is to increase awareness of flooding , provide additional flood related resources, and provide a public platform to voice concerns in order to promote lasting solutions.

According to the Courier Journal Kentucky has some of the highest concentrations of community flood risk in the country, according to a report.
Almost 249,000 properties in the commonwealth "have greater than a 26% chance of being severely affected by flooding over the next 30 years," the "Third National Flood Risk Assessment" says. That's about 13% of all properties statewide, putting Kentucky in the top four of all states in terms of flood risk. Read the article.

Leah's Alarm created by Barren County High School

"Students at Barren County High School have developed an alarm system that will warn users about high water in honor of a student Leah Carter, who died when her car was swept away by flood waters as she tried to cross a bridge on Lyon Road in Monroe County" Read the article.

Flood Factor

Flood factor is one of the most informative flood based websites. It provides vital information that can help you identify the risk of flooding in your specific area. Also, it provides future projections based on science. Visit the site.
